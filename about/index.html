Machine learning/Astronomy PhD student with Dr Anna Scaife @ University of Manchester. University of Oxford MPhys graduate.

Research and PhD Work

I am working on building robust machine learning systems to process the vast deluge of data (exabyte scale) we are about to receive from next-generation telescopes. Since this much data cannot be curated by humans, we need AI that can deal with messy, real-world data with few labels and many possible anomalies.

My research has included semi-supervised learning and use of unlabelled data for improved classification in the astronomy domain, bespoke contrastive learning applied to astronomical data-sets and use of generative algorithms to simulate astronomical objects. I enjoy sharing my work with both computer scientists and astronomers and have published my work/given talks at NeurIPS, EAS and NAM.

Broader Interests

I’d like to continue applying cutting edge machine learning techniques to open problems and new data-sets which require domain specific improvements. I’m always looking for new opportunities and challenges to broaden my expertise and apply what I know to build better solutions for high impact applications. I strongly believe in building non-brittle AI systems which can deal with the unpredictable nature of the real world rather than chasing marginal state-of-the-art results on benchmarking data-sets.

Outside of my work I’m passionate about enduro/downhill mountain biking, snowboarding/skiing, basketball and adventures in the wilderness.
